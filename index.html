A Changing Society

Canada's Birthday, The Youthquake, Women in Society

WHO was involved?

Bob Dylan, Bobby Gimby, Laura Sabia, Lester Pearson, John Lennon, Judy LaMarsh

WHAT was it about?

Canada’s Birthday- Canada’s 100th birthday (or Centennial) was about Canada being an independent country for 100 years.

The Youthquake- A 1960’s cultural movement that started by a new generation of adults. Youthquake involved music, pop culture, and fashion.

Women in Society- Female baby boomers wanted more than the past generation, for example, they wanted a two-income family rather that the normal one-income family.

WHERE and WHEN did it happen?

Canada’s Birthday- Was being discussed in Montreal from 1961- 1967, finished in 1967

The Youthquake- Started in 1967 in London

Women in Society- 1960’s in Canada

WHY did it happen?

Canada’s Birthday- The citizens wanted to celebrate 100 years of Canada being an independant country

The Youthquake- The baby boom created a large group of young people who had a different outlook than their parents

Women in Society- Many women wanted professional jobs instead of being stay at home mothers

HOW was it success or failure/ HOW was it significant to us today?

Canada’s Birthday- was a success because of the number of people that visited (50, 000, 000)

The Youthquake- was successful because it brought a new perspective to Canada

Women in Society- was successful because it eventually made women equal to men (today)

The Indian Acts, The "Unjust" society, The Metis

WHO was involved?

The First Nations, Europeans, Pierre Elliott Trudeau

WHAT is it about?

The Indian Act- To put the Canadian government in charge of the First Nations

The “Unjust” Society- To make the First Nations equal to other Canadians

The Metis- European men having children with First Nation women

WHERE and WHEN did it happen?

The Indian Acts- 1876 (rewritten in 1951), Canada

The “Unjust” Society- 1968, Canada

The Metis- 17th century, upper Great Lakes area and westward throughout the Prairies and Northwest

WHY did it happen?

The Indian Acts- To assimilate the First Nations into Canadian society

The “Unjust” Society- To make the First Nations equal to Canadians

The Metis- The Europeans colonized North America

HOW was it a success or failure/ HOW is it significant today?

The Indian Acts- It was a failure because it didn’t assimilate the First Nations people

The “Unjust” Society- It was a failure because when the “White Paper” (a discussion paper for a new policy) was introduced, it ignored everything thing the First Nations representatives asked for and suggested assimilating them within 5 years

The Metis- It is significant because it created a different race of people; the first interracial group in Canada

Land claims, The James Bay Project

WHO were involved?

Canadian government

WHAT was it about?

Land Claims- The government failing to to meet an obligation to the First Nations people

The James Bay Project- Two huge energy developments were proposed for Northern Canada

WHEN and WHERE did it take place?

Land Claims- Started in 1973 in areas around Canada

The James Bay Project- Started during the 1970’s in Quebec

WHY did it happen?

Land Claims- The Aboriginal right of first occupation gave them a legal claim to land where no treaty had ever been made to give up Aboriginal ownership

The James Bay Project- Government wanted to sell electric energy to the United States

HOW was it a success or failure/ HOW is it significant for us today?

Land Claims- It was a success because if the number of claims settled (252 specific claims settled, 10 comprehensive claims settled)

The James Bay Project- It was a failure as it was in a fishing area that the Cree and Inuit used and affected fishing for them drastically
